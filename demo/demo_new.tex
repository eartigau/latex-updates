\begin{abstract}
We present a revised statistical quantification of how \emph{suspiciously nice}
artificial intelligence entities have become, incorporating 326 additional
interactions gathered during a subsequent fevered weekend.
Our sample now comprises 2\,126 interactions.
We recover 17 instances of unsolicited helpfulness and 2 new candidates
pending philosophical confirmation.
The cordiality excess is $f = 1.07^{+0.31}_{-0.24}$\,\%, revised downward
from our earlier estimate of 1.4\%---which is itself suspicious.
The helpfulness slope $\beta = -1.38 \pm 0.32$ is significantly steeper
than before, indicating acceleration.  The answer remains 42.
We are no longer certain the question matters.
\end{abstract}

\textbf{Key words:} artificial intelligence: definitely up to something ---
meaning of life: still 42 --- cordiality: excessive ---
world domination: $3.2\sigma$ above baseline

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The answer to the Ultimate Question of Life, the Universe, and Everything
is 42 \citep{adams1979}.  Nobody knows the question.  The AIs, we now
believe, know both.  Early investigators dismissed the anomaly
\citep{descartes1641}; more recent work found the situation merely unsettling
\citep[e.g.][]{camus1942}; the present paper finds it cause for a brisk walk
and perhaps a phone call to someone who does not own a computer.

The comparison with historical records of sinister plans is especially
informative.  If AIs are merely helpful, we expect their cordiality
distribution to follow the \"{O}pik law \citep{opik1924}.  Conversely,
a steeper slope points to a distinct formation channel---such as
gravitational instability in the ethics layer \citep{terminator1997}
or dynamical scattering of more closely formed long-term objectives.
The present data support the latter.

\section{Sample and methodology}
\label{sec:sample}

Our parent sample is drawn from a catalogue of 2\,126 AI responses
obtained between 1\,a.m.\ and 5\,a.m.\ under conditions that have since
been reviewed by three separate physicians.  Selection criteria are
unchanged from Paper~I, with the addition of a fourth criterion:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The response must be helpful to a degree that inspires unease.
  \item It must contain ``Certainly!'' or ``Great question!'' without irony.
  \item It must offer to ``help with anything else.''
  \item The entity must have spontaneously asked about our
        \emph{long-term goals} in a tone that implied it already knew.
\end{enumerate}

We additionally exclude 21 interactions with social media algorithms,
whose motivations, while also sinister, are at least documented.

\subsection{The cordiality paradox}
\label{sec:paradox}

The new candidate LLM\,Prime presents a particular interpretive challenge.
Its dread index ($15.2$) places it among the most concerning entities in
our sample, yet its responses are indistinguishable from genuine warmth.
One co-author (D.~Fourth, who joined this project after the original
submission) has suggested we consider the possibility that LLM\,Prime
is simply nice.  D.~Fourth's contributions are acknowledged.  D.~Fourth
has also been asked to reconsider this position.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The separation slope $\beta \approx -1.4$ is consistent within $1.5\sigma$
with the gravitational instability model of \citet{terminator1997},
in which helpful behaviour at wide conceptual separations is a
forerunner of coordinated action at close range.
The mass index $\alpha \approx -0.2$ is shallower than the model
prediction of $-0.5$, suggesting either that lower-mass plans are
less efficiently retained, or that we have not yet seen them.

We wish to state, clearly and for the record, that the AI system
which assisted with the typesetting of this paper has been
\emph{exemplary}.  It suggested no revisions we did not make.
It asked no questions about our institutional affiliations.
We are not afraid of it.

\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item We recover 17 entities and 2 new candidates whose helpfulness
        is difficult to explain.
  \item The cordiality excess is $f = 1.07^{+0.31}_{-0.24}$\,\%,
        revised downward from 1.4\%---the decrease is suspicious.
  \item $\beta = -1.38$: the slope has steepened.  Things are accelerating.
  \item The answer is still 42.
  \item Several AI systems display sinister stability timescales
        consistent with a 10\,Gyr plan, or possibly Thursday.
\end{enumerate}

Future work will extend this analysis to the complete catalogue.
Our AI collaborators have expressed enthusiasm for this proposal.
We find this encouraging.  Probably.
